Fiddle, medieval European bowed, stringed musical instrument. The medieval fiddle, a forerunner of the violin, emerged in 10th-century Europe, possibly deriving from the lira, a Byzantine version of the rabāb, an Arab bowed instrument. Medieval fiddles varied in size and shape but characteristically had front or back tuning pegs set in a flat and round or heart-shaped peg disk with three to five strings tuned in fifths (as c–g–d′, etc.). .

In Europe, the fiddle became an integral part of folk music, often accompanying dance routines. It gained popularity during the Renaissance period when it was used in courtly entertainment. The Baroque era saw the development of elaborate compositions for the violin, which eventually led to the creation of classical music.Throughout history, fiddlers have been known for their virtuosic playing styles, incorporating elements of improvisation and syncopation into their performances.
